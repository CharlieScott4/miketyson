The Career Of Mike Tyson

Biography

Mike Tyson, full name Michael Gerald Tyson, also known as Iron Mike,, was born on June 30, 1966, in Brooklyn, New York. Mike Tyson is an American boxer who became the youngest heavyweight champion in history at only 20 years old. At an early age Mike Tyson was in various street gangs until his boxing potential was noticed by Bobby Stewart, he would then send him over to Cus D'Amato, who became his legal guardian and his trainer. Tyson was short and squat being 5 feet 11 inches and weighed 218 pounds. He didn't appear as intimidating to his opponents but he would quickly suprise them with his speed. Later on in his career he was accused of rape and spent 1992-1995 in jail, once released he immediately returned to boxing. Tyson retired in 2005 after his last professional fight on June 11, 2005, leaving him with 44 KO wins and 5 KO losses.
